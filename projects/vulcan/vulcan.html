Vulcan (2021)

Final prototype of Vulcan

Description

This is the projcet from my product design senior capstone class. Vulcan is a butane powered, hand held, wire-to-wire soldering device. This was my team's final project for my capstone product design class. I was responsible for many parts of the solder feed mechanism (solder feed tubes, axels for the gears, pulley, and more) as well as general team dynamics and project management.

Because this work comes from my capstone product design class, a lot of work went into prototypes that were not the final version of this project. At one point, we were exploring a system for putting out electric vehicle fires and I was responsible for some of those early prototypes. Before settling on using fuel for the soldering mechanism, I spent a lot of time designing a PCB for the device to be electrically powered. Ultimetly, fuel power was a better decision for the device because of enegry density.

Lessons Learned

I learned a lot about working with other people and general project management. I also learned a decent amount on rapid prototyping and how to effectively budget. I also got significantly better at making shafts with slots on the lathe.
