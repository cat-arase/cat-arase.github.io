3D Printers & Early Design (2012-2018)

Pencil holder made for shop orientation

Key Achievements

  • Learned laser engraver
  • Bandaw, sander, drillpress safety
  • Combining multiple processes

Description

In order to gain shop access my freshman year, I had to go through the Makerlodge program. A part of this program was building a pencil holder. This pencil holder was cut on a bandsaw. The holes are done with a drill press. The holder was then sanded. The red gear was laser cut and the black gear was 3D printed.

Lessons Learned

The Makerlodge used the laser engraver to mark the spots of the holes for the gears. I had never seen this done before, but thought it was brilliant to make sure the holes were in the correct spots.
